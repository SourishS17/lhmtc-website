Fundraising

Clay Pigeon Shoot

Some of our members took part in a Charity Clay Pigeon Shoot at Great Massingham. Great Fun and over £250.00 was raised.

The Three Peaks Challenge

A group of intrepid Gentlemen decided to take on the challenge of conquering the summits of Ben Nevis, Scafell Pike and Snowdon - in a day! After hiking 25 miles and climbing up to 10,000 feet, they emerged very tired - but victorious! Over £3,000 was raised for the LHMTC - well done lads!

The Huntingdon 10k Run

One of our Mums took part in this race to raise much-needed funds for the group. The event also marked a personal milestone!

Christmas Music Marathon

The LHMTC staged its 'Christmas Music Marathon'. Starting at the Vancouver Quarter, King's Lynn at 12.00pm, the group moved on to Hardwick and finished singing at 7.30pm!

Fundraising is the key to keep us going. If you have any ideas on fundraising events, please do get in touch!

But these alone are not enough - so please support us by sponsoring activities if you're a business looking to help theatre, or donate if you're an individual who wants to change lives!

Simon Rowe

Jamie John has been associated with LHMTC since 2008, when he came on board as both cast member and Dance Workshop Leader/Choreographer. Jamie’s acting and performing credits have been many and varied, from ‘Little Britain’ and the ‘Seven Dwarfs’ documentary, to the ’Harry Potter’ series of films. In more recent times, Jamie has found fame and notoriety with his alter-ego, ’Miss DQ’ - the UK’s only dwarf Drag Queen and is much in demand all over the world. 2018 saw Miss DQ own the stage at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival as well as many UK and International Pride events and over the 2018 Christmas period Jamie was treading the boards at the London Palladium alongside Dawn French and Julian Clary in ‘Snow White’. Jamie has also recently signed up with a London modelling agency and for most of 2019, he performed nationwide with the ’Lady Boys of Bangkok’. 2020 brought a halt to all performances in the entertainment industry, but Jamie has some big plans for 2021 and beyond.

Adam has appeared in many diverse plays, toured with the Royal Shakespeare Company and has been involved in various dramas and international films, such as 'Mistresses', 'Doctor Who', 'Miranda', 'The Whale', 'The Saint', 'Tyrant' and more recently, he has been filming in Canada.

Warwick has starred in many high-profile films including 'Return of the Jedi', 'The Phantom Menace', 'Willow', 'Chronicles of Narnia', 'Hitch Hiker's Guide to the Galaxy' and of course playing the role of Professor Flitwick in the 'Harry Potter' series. His TV credits include, 'Life's Too Short', 'Perspectives', 'Weekend Escapes', 'Top Gear', 'An Idiot Abroad' and he is soon to be seen hosting the revived series 'Celebrity Squares'. Warwick is a keen charity supporter and in 2013, appeared in a Comic Relief episode 'The British Bake-off', winning the title of 'Comic Relief Star Baker' for the episode. Warwick's stage work CV is equally as colourful and it has led him to become the founder of the 'Reduced Height Theatre Company', with their first production tour of 'See How They Run' in 2014. Warwick is the co-founder of 'Willow Management Ltd' - a talent agency who represent actors under 5 feet tall and also, actors over 7 foot tall!

Simon is very well known around the County of Norfolk and beyond. For many years, being a lively wake-up call to many over the airwaves of KL.FM and the Breakfast Show, he now runs his own radio station - 'Radio West Norfolk', broadcasting online to the same local audience!
